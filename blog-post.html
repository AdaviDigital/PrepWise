10 High-Yield Topics WAEC Examiners Repeat Every Year

Published July 2026 · By the PrepWise Editorial Team

Every year, thousands of candidates lose easy marks on WAEC not because the questions were unfair, but because certain topics were skipped during revision. Our team analysed the last ten years of WAEC papers across the most-registered subjects to find the topics that come up again and again.

1. Mathematics: Mensuration and Geometry

Questions on area, volume and geometric construction appear in almost every paper. Practising past questions on these topics alone can secure several guaranteed marks.

2. English Language: Comprehension and Lexis & Structure

Comprehension passages and structure questions are consistently the largest scoring sections. Regular timed reading practice makes the biggest difference here.

3. Physics: Waves and Electricity

These two topics anchor a significant share of the objective and theory sections most years.

4. Chemistry: Stoichiometry and Organic Chemistry

Calculation-based stoichiometry questions and organic chemistry naming conventions are examined almost every sitting.

How to use this list

Rather than reading everything equally, spend your remaining revision weeks weighting these topics higher, then use PrepWise's AI Exam Predictor to confirm which of them are your personal weak points before exam day.
